Guide to Circuit Simulation in KiCad

November 09, 2025 | by Mongare Joshua

Introduction

Circuit simulation is one of the most important skills for every electrical and electronics engineer. KiCad, being one of the best open-source tools, offers a powerful SPICE-based simulator that lets you test and validate designs before creating a PCB.

Why Simulation Matters
  • Reduces hardware failures
  • Helps validate component behavior
  • Saves cost and prototyping time
  • Improves design accuracy
Steps to Simulate Your First Circuit
  1. Install KiCad and create a new project.
  2. Open the schematic editor and place basic components.
  3. Assign SPICE models to active components.
  4. Wire the circuit and add power sources.
  5. Insert simulation commands such as transient or DC sweep.
  6. Run the simulation and analyze the waveforms.
Final Thoughts

KiCad gives engineers a complete workflow from schematic to PCB. Starting with simulation ensures your design is ready for real-world testing. Once you master this, you’ll design faster and make fewer mistakes.

Top Open-Source Tools for Engineers

November 15, 2025 | by Mongare Joshua

Why Open-Source?

Open-source tools give engineers freedom, customization, and cost-effective solutions. These tools are widely used in academia, industry, and hobby projects because of their flexibility.

Top Tools You Should Know
  • KiCad: PCB design and SPICE simulation.
  • FreeCAD: 3D modeling and mechanical design.
  • GNU Radio: SDR development and RF signal processing.
  • Python + NumPy/SciPy: Scientific computing.
  • Arduino IDE: Embedded systems development.
  • Inkscape: Diagram and vector graphics for documentation.
Benefits for Engineers
  • No licensing restrictions
  • Strong global community support
  • Regular updates and improvements
  • High compatibility and interoperability
Closing Thoughts

Mastering open-source tools expands your capability and helps you build high-quality engineering projects without paying for expensive software. They are especially valuable for students and self-taught developers.

Windows vs Linux: Which is Better for Developers?

November 22, 2025 | by Mongare Joshua

Overview

Windows and Linux are the two most widely used operating systems in development. Each provides unique advantages depending on your workflow, tools, and project type.

Why Developers Choose Linux
  • Native support for programming tools (Git, Python, Bash, GCC)
  • Better performance for servers and containers
  • Higher stability and customizability
  • Massive open-source support
Why Developers Choose Windows
  • Great for design, gaming, and productivity apps
  • Best compatibility with commercial software
  • Windows Subsystem for Linux (WSL) bridges the gap
  • Easy for beginners
Which Should You Use?

If you want raw performance, server-friendly tools, or full customization, Linux wins easily. If you want compatibility, comfort, or heavy GUI apps like Adobe or SolidWorks, Windows is the better choice.

Final Verdict

In reality, many engineers dual-boot or use WSL to get the best of both worlds. Choose based on your workflow, but understanding both systems makes you a stronger engineer.
